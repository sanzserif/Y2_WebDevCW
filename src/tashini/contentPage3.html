Musical instruments of Palladianism period

Throughout the 18th century, Palladianism was a fashionable architectural style in England. It was characterized by an emphasis on symmetry, proportion, and simplicity as well as a return to classical ideals. The classical style had an impact on musical instruments throughout this time period as well. During the Palladianism era, the following musical instruments were common:

• Fortepiano: A form of piano that gained popularity in the late 18th century was the fortepiano. It had a softer, more delicate sound and was smaller than a modern piano. The fortepiano was a favorite instrument of composers like Mozart and Beethoven and was frequently utilized in chamber music.

 • Harpsichord: A common keyboard instrument during the Palladianism era was the harpsichord. By using a quill or plectrum to pluck the strings, it had a distinctive tone. Baroque music frequently used the harpsichord, which was a favorite instrument of artists like Bach and Handel.

 • Violin: A common stringed instrument during the Palladianism era was the violin. It was a favorite of composers like Vivaldi and Haydn and frequently employed in chamber music and orchestras. By bowing the strings, the violin could produce a unique sound.

 • Flute: A common woodwind instrument during the Palladianism era was the flute. It was a favorite of composers like Mozart and Haydn and frequently employed in chamber music and orchestras. By blowing air into the flute, one could produce a loud, distinct sound.

 • Cello: A common stringed instrument during the Palladianism era was the cello. It was a favorite of composers like Bach and Beethoven and frequently utilized in chamber music and orchestras. The deep, rich sound of the cello was produced by bowing the strings.

 In general, the Palladianism era was a time of remarkable innovation and ingenuity in musical instrument creation. Many of the popular musical instruments from this era are still in use today.
